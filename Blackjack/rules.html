Game Rules

  • Each player starts with two cards.
  • Aces can count as 1 or 11, face cards are worth 10, and all other cards are worth their face value.
  • You can choose to "hit" (take another card) or "stand" (keep your current hand).
  • If your total exceeds 21, you "bust" and lose the game.
  • The dealer must hit until their total is 17 or higher.
  • If you have a higher total than the dealer without busting, you win!
  • If you tie with the dealer, it's a push (no one wins).
  • If the dealer busts, you win automatically.

Scoring

  • Blackjack (an Ace and a 10-value card) pays 3:2.
  • Winning hands pay even money (1:1).
  • If you tie with the dealer, your bet is returned (push).
  • If you bust, you lose your bet.

Additional Rules

  • You can double down after the initial deal, but you must double your bet.
  • Splitting pairs allows you to play two hands, but you must place an additional bet.
  • Insurance is offered if the dealer's face-up card is an Ace; it pays 2:1 if the dealer has blackjack.
  • Some casinos allow surrendering, where you can forfeit half your bet if you think you will lose.
